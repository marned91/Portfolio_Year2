About Me

I’m a frontend developer currently studying at Noroff in a two-year program (2023–2025), where I focus on building user-friendly and responsive web applications.

I’m passionate about clean, efficient code and enjoy turning design into smooth, accessible user experiences!

MY Background

I’m currently in my final year of the Frontend Development program at Noroff (2023–2025), where I’ve built a strong foundation in JavaScript, Tailwind, and Vite. The program emphasizes modern workflows and project-based learning, and so far I’ve completed several real-world projects involving APIs, accessibility, testing, routing, and CMS. I’m now transitioning into React and our final open-source collaboration.

Before making the switch to tech, I worked as a CRM Manager at Jottacloud, where I focused on improving the customer journey for existing users. Working closely with developers sparked my interest in frontend development, and eventually led me to pursue coding full-time — combining my background in creativity, innovation, and business development with a strong focus on user experience.

Why Frontend

I chose frontend development because I wanted to move from planning and managing projects — including working on the customer journey — to actually building the user-facing experiences myself. While I enjoyed the strategic aspects of my previous roles, I missed being hands-on and part of the technical creation process.

Frontend development lets me combine structure, logic, and user experience through code. I'm especially excited to continue growing with tools like React, and I'm motivated by projects that make a real difference — whether in Norwegian tech, education, healthcare, sustainability, animal welfare.

My Goals

I aim to begin my career as a frontend developer, ideally contributing to a product or tech team where I can make a meaningful impact. My focus is on continuing to grow my skills through real-world experience and becoming a confident, collaborative developer.

I appreciate being part of a team while valuing my moments of solitude, which allows me to thrive in both social and quiet settings.
